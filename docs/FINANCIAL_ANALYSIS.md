# Financial and Environmental Impact Analysis

## 1. Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) Model
The Phase-1 deployment targets immediate controllable loads with a total capital requirement of **Rs. 8.44 Crores**.

| Budget Category | Allocation | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Infrastructure (Solar) | Rs. 6.24 Cr | Installation of 1.2 MWp elevated parking canopy system. |
| Automation & Controls | Rs. 1.15 Cr | Smart relays, occupancy sensors, and room-level smart meters. |
| Circular Utility Assets | Rs. 0.60 Cr | Reverse Vending Machines (RVMs) and solar-integrated cooling nodes. |
| Logic & Software Layer | Rs. 0.45 Cr | OS deployment, mobile integration, and credit ledger development. |

## 2. Operational Revenue and Savings
### 2.1 Energy Cost Reduction
Based on a baseline consumption of 26.7 GWh/year and an average industrial tariff of Rs. 6.30/kWh, the OS is projected to deliver:
- **Gross Annual Savings**: Rs. 1.81 Crores/year (Reduction of 2.87 million kWh).
- **Maintenance Optimization**: Estimated 15% reduction in HVAC wear-and-tear through intelligent cycling.

### 2.2 Ancillary Revenue Streams
- **Advertising Revenue**: Deployment of digital signage on RVM and EV charging nodes (Projected Rs. 4.5 Lakhs/year).
- **Recycling Material Recovery**: Sale of recovered PET and aluminum (Projected Rs. 1.8 Lakhs/year).

## 3. Return on Investment (ROI)
- **Net Annual Benefit**: Rs. 1.69 Crores/year (Adjusted for O&M costs).
- **Simple Payback Period**: 5.0 Years.
- **10-Year NPV**: Estimated at Rs. 12.4 Crores (at 8% discount rate).

## 4. Environmental Impact (Carbon Lifecycle)
Applying the Central Electricity Authority (CEA) Version 19.0 grid-emission factor of 0.716 tCO2/MWh for the Indian Grid:
- **Annual Scope 2 Reduction**: 2,055 tCO2e (Phase-1).
- **Cumulative 24-Month Target**: 5,141 tCO2e (Phases 1-3).
- **Environmental Equivalence**: Equivalent to sequestering the carbon output of approximately 245,000 mature trees annually.
