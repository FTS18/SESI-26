# Governance, Risk Management, and Implementation Roadmap

## 1. Institutional Governance Framework
The Arasaka Energy OS is governed by a three-tiered administrative structure to ensure operational accountability and data integrity.

### 1.1 Administrative Oversight
- **Sustainability Steering Committee**: Responsible for reviewing quarterly energy scorecards and approving subsequent phase funding.
- **Facilities Management Unit**: Oversees physical asset maintenance and reviews 'Anomaly Exception Reports' generated by the OS.
- **Timetable Office**: Ensures synchronization between academic bookings and the OS control schedules.

### 1.2 Autonomous Audit Protocols
Every command executed by the Arasaka logic engine is recorded in the **Autonomous Audit Log**. This log is immutable and provides the following telemetry for every decision:
- **Timestamp and Location ID**
- **Input Data** (e.g., Solar Yield < 40%, Occupancy = 0)
- **Action Taken** (e.g., HVAC Throttled to 60%)
- **Justification Code** (e.g., SOLAR_PRIORITY_LOW_YIELD)

## 2. 24-Month Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Controllable Infrastructure (Months 0–8)
- Deployment of smart classroom controls and parking canopy solar assets.
- Objective: Establishing the foundational OS logic and verifying the initial 10% grid reduction.

### Phase 2: Residential and Living Loads (Months 6–16)
- Integration of hostel infrastructure, including geyser scheduling and corridor occupancy lighting.
- Objective: Implementing the credit-based student engagement model and expanding the telemetry footprint.

### Phase 3: High-Intensity Engineering Optimization (Months 12–24)
- Advanced chiller plant optimization and server-room cooling modulation.
- Objective: Achieving the final 26.9% cumulative imported-grid reduction target.

## 3. Risk Mitigation and Contingency
| Risk Category | Mitigation Protocol |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Operational Disruption** | Implementation of 'Comfort-Guard' logic that prevents set-point deviations beyond ±1.5°C from the institutional standard. |
| **Hardware Reliability** | Weekly automated health checks of all IoT gateways and PV inverters with immediate facility alerts. |
| **Stakeholder Resistance** | Use of real-time leaderboard transparency to foster inter-departmental competition and social accountability. |
| **Target Variance** | Use of the 'Contingency Shelf' (pre-designed rooftop solar expansions) if reduction targets slip below 90% of the base-case model. |
